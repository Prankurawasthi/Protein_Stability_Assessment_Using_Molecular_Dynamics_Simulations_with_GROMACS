# Project Report

## Title

Molecular Dynamics Simulation of Lysozyme in Water Using GROMACS

## Background

Molecular dynamics simulation is a computational technique used to investigate the structural stability and dynamic behavior of biomolecules under physiological conditions.

## Objective

To perform a complete molecular dynamics simulation workflow for lysozyme in an aqueous environment using GROMACS.

## Methodology

1. Downloaded Lysozyme structure (PDB ID: 1AKI).
2. Generated topology using GROMACS force field.
3. Created cubic simulation box.
4. Solvated system with TIP3P water molecules.
5. Neutralized system with counter ions.
6. Performed energy minimization.
7. Conducted NVT equilibration at 300 K.
8. Conducted NPT equilibration at 1 bar pressure.
9. Performed production molecular dynamics simulation.
10. Evaluated trajectory using RMSD and Radius of Gyration analysis.

## Results

The workflow demonstrated the standard protocol used for biomolecular molecular dynamics simulations.

Key analyses included:

* RMSD for structural stability assessment
* RMSF for residue flexibility analysis
* Radius of Gyration for compactness evaluation
* Temperature stabilization during NVT
* Pressure and density stabilization during NPT
